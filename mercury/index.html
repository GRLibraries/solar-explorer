Mercury is the smallest planet and the closest one to the Sun. It zips around the Sun in just 88 Earth days—making it the speedster of the solar system! Despite being so close to the Sun, it’s not the hottest planet because it has almost no atmosphere to trap heat.

Mercury has some wild temperature swings—from scorching 430°C (800°F) during the day to freezing -180°C (-290°F) at night. The planet is named after the Roman messenger god, which fits its speedy orbit. And fun fact: a day on Mercury (sunrise to sunrise) lasts 176 Earth days!
